    In the Midst of Unpredictable Fate, Finding One's Own Poetry: A Materialist's Exploration of Astrological Models

    Personal psychological preparation before delving into astrology.
    March 7, 2026 | Translations:

    I am an objective materialist. I believe in science, rationality, and empiricism.

    However, I am also wary: simply idolizing science is also a form of “superstition” — where “superstition” here refers to unquestioningly accepting any theory in its entirety.

    My attitude towards the ultimate laws of life is: I believe there is a fundamental “Dao”, but all human theories (such as science, theology, philosophy, psychology, metaphysics) are models approximating it. Some models are rudimentary and cannot withstand scrutiny; others are precise and irrefutable. But across many disciplines, researchers, driven by curiosity and imagination, attempt to build models they believe in.

    And astrology, in my view, is merely one such model.

    Part One: Why I “Believe” in Astrology?#

    The connection between birth charts and people, personality and destiny, holds a mysterious allure for me. I often think of the Twelve Beauties’ judgments in Dream of the Red Chamber: Each person’s character and fate, in fact, already carries implicit meanings.

    Or the setting in Pixar’s film Soul:

    Before we come to Earth, souls have spent time in different ‘seminars’. Selfishness, courage, peace… the duration of our stay constitutes our innate personality foundation. Afterwards, we go to the ‘You Seminar’ to find our ‘Spark’, that passion is the key to a successful rebirth.

    For me, astrology is more like a “factory settings manual”: It’s not a verdict on destiny, but a reference for personality archetypes.

    Part Two: My Curiosity and Exploration#

    Like most people, I started to learn about astrology when I fell into emotional confusion. Questions like ‘Does he like me?’ and ‘Are we compatible?’ pushed me towards astrological interpretations.

    I’ve watched almost all of Teacher Tang Qiyang’s videos and learned that everyone has not only a sun sign, but also the moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto. Everyone has a unique birth chart.

    [Model Definition]: Birth Chart

    It is a snapshot of the celestial positions at the moment of a person’s birth. Because celestial bodies are constantly moving, a difference of a few minutes can lead to completely different implications.

    This “unprecedented and never-to-be-repeated” explanation aligns with my worldview. However, when I tried to search for professional literature (such as Google Scholar or astronomical findings), I didn’t find any convincing arguments.

    Thus, I encountered a common paradox faced by rationalists confronting metaphysics: both a desire to explore and a caution against getting lost.

    Part Three: My “Irrational” Investment#

    Teacher Yang Zhao is a historian I admire, and also the pride of us Arians. I spent a considerable amount of money to enroll in his new course this year, ‘The Logical Astrology Class’.

    This marks my formal exploration into astrology, which is ’not officially recognized’. Interestingly, I’m reluctant to spend money on things I deeply believe in, like AI and psychology, yet I invest in metaphysics. I often reflect: Is this a form of escape from the pressures of academic pursuits and work?

    But emotionally, I subtly hold an unproven belief: Perhaps, even in undecided chaos, there can be imaginative and rational discussions.

    However, learning astrology seems to be inescapable from a dilemma:

    1. Is this the most accurate interpretation for me?
    2. How should I view this verdict of fate?

    I still remember the shock of watching Rebecca in The Accidental Story say before her death that her birthday was not that day at all. The entire logic of the drama was built on the premise that the two female leads shared the same birthday and personality, thus their lives would intertwine. Unexpectedly, it was revealed at the end that the foundation of this premise was false; the two were not even the same zodiac sign. Rebecca didn’t know her actual birth date; her parents just arbitrarily wrote one.

    Or, for example, I was born on the cusp of Aries and Taurus, always considered myself a Taurus, and then suddenly discovered I was actually an Aries. All my previous rationalizations seemed ridiculous.

    Part Four: Why Am I ‘Afraid to Delve Deeper’?#

    Although I believe astrology has a certain explanatory power, I always keep a distance from it:

    • If I see misfortune (e.g., Saturn return): I’m afraid of being anxious all day and unable to enjoy the present.
    • If I see good omens: I’m afraid of setting expectations too high and then being anxious about squandering my luck.

    I often contradictorily hope: to meet a very effective master for guidance, but not one who is so accurate that they predict everything correctly. Because I still want to preserve the joy and courage of exploration in a state of ignorance. Even if my life has indeed been pre-programmed, I still want to retain the illusion that I arrived here seemingly through my own choices and agency.

    I also thought of the setting in the Foundation Series:

    To save galactic civilization, shorten the time from its decline to its resurgence, all scientists responsible for preserving and advancing human civilization and technology were placed in the First Foundation. However, one type of scientist: psychohistorians (psychologists who use mathematical statistics to predict the overall fate of humanity) had to be hidden in the Second Foundation. Because once those being predicted knew their fate, they would resist or modify it, causing the prediction to fail.

    What I yearn for, perhaps, is to maintain the distance between the First Foundation and the Second Foundation: even if life has already been coded, I want to preserve the illusion, an illusion as if I arrived here precisely through my own choices and agency.

    Part Five: If I Knew My Destiny, How Would I Live?#

    Although I am still far from understanding birth charts and building my own models. If I truly possessed extraordinary talent and could foresee fate, how would I maintain vitality? I thought of Xiangling from Dream of the Red Chamber. She was someone whose tragic fate was accurately predicted very early on. Her father, Zhen Shiyin, was fortunate enough to encounter a bald-headed monk and a lame Taoist priest, both in a dream and in reality—people who could truly see through destiny. But what they spoke of was a bitter fate that we desperately try to avoid. Zhen Shiyin’s daughter, whom he held in his arms, was described as ‘having life but no luck, a burden to her parents’. Later, after the Lantern Festival, Yinglian was abducted by human traffickers. Zhen Shiyin and his wife faced a series of misfortunes, and their family fortunes declined. Yinglian was passed through several hands before finally meeting a reliable fiancé, but before the marriage, she was taken by the brute Xue Pan and kept in his household, where she was treated indifferently. Xiangling’s fate was indeed as tumultuous and difficult as the bald-headed monk had foretold. But what moves me is Xiangling’s days in the Grand View Garden, learning poetry tirelessly with Daiyu and Baochai. No matter how her background or destiny ravaged her, she always held onto her pursuit and steadfastness for her own life. Regardless of her identity, she eventually found her way to her rightful place; destiny still cherished her.

    In those moments of poetic obsession, she was no one’s maid; she was simply a person fully enjoying life.

    Adversity and prosperity are the general trend, difficult for human power to resist; but our attitude towards destiny is always our own choice.

    Conclusion#

    Currently, I maintain an ‘ambiguous’ relationship with astrology: when I see favorable descriptions, I keep them as positive prompts for action; when I see unfavorable predictions, I quietly remind myself that fortune and misfortune are interdependent, and challenges are not necessarily bad.

    I want to maintain an open mind to explore my personality traits, but I don’t want to become a fortune-telling immortal. I subtly hope to foresee the future, but I believe even more: Destiny itself has no good or bad; fortune and misfortune are all creations of chance.

    What’s important is to preserve that courage, like Xiangling. In the midst of unpredictable fate, find your own poetry.
