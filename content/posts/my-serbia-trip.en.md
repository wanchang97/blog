# General Travel Tips for Serbia

## üõÇ Entry and Visa
**Visa-free for Chinese citizens**. The process is extremely smooth. Basically no queuing, no difficulty, just a stamp for entry. Currently, Serbia is a non-Schengen country in the Balkans, so you don't need a Schengen visa to fly directly from China.

---

## üì∂ Network and SIM Cards
### Domestic Carrier International Roaming - Not Recommended
1. **Signal Issues:** Roaming signal is unstable and often degrades.
2. **"The Wall" Exists:** International roaming from China still blocks Google Maps, ChatGPT, or Gemini, which is fatal for independent travel (maps/translation).
So, it is better to connect to airport WiFi temporarily and buy a local SIM card.

### International Travel eSIM - Flexible but Slightly Expensive
Suitable for multi-country trips. Note that some Chinese phones only support dual physical SIMs and no eSIM, while US versions might be eSIM only. Please check your phone compatibility.

### Buying Local SIM/eSIM
* **Where to buy:** **Yettel**, **A1**, and other carrier counters are located at the baggage claim in the airport. Prices are transparent, no scams, and activated with your passport.
* **Packages (Feb 2026):**
    * **Serbia Only:** 
    For example, Yettel offers 100GB for 15 days (700 RSD) or 50GB for 7 days (500 RSD), suitable for tourists.

## üöå Transportation

* **Airport Shuttle (A1):** Extra cost (approx. 400 RSD/person). Direct to city center. Old vehicles but very fast.
* **City Bus:** **Free from Jan 1, 2025** (including tourists). High frequency, crowded but convenient.
* **Intercity Bus:** Google Maps often fails to show intercity transit, only driving/walking routes. Dozens of private companies (Lasta, Jugoprevoz, Fudeks, etc.) operate buses and don't share real-time data with Google.
**Alternatives:**
1. [BAS Official Booking Site](https://www.bas.rs/sr/autobuske-karte)
2. [Local Bus Site (Polazak)](https://www.polazak.rs/sr/). You can book online, but for operators like Lasta, you might need to pay a platform fee (approx. 500 RSD) at the [BAS station](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Belgrade+Bus+Station/@44.8081721,20.40194,4036m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m10!1m2!2m1!1sBAS!3m6!1s0x475a7aacb8ca4d5d:0x7db94704474c1529!8m2!3d44.8058417!4d20.416284!15sCgNCQVNaBSIDYmFzkgELYnVzX2NvbXBhbnmaASNDaFpEU1VoTk1HOW5TMFZKUTBGblNVTmtkbDlNZEdKQkVBReABAPoBBAgAEEQ!16s%2Fg%2F1tdkql6x?entry=ttu&g_ep=EgoyMDI2MDIwMy4wIKXMDSoASAFQAw%3D%3D). Buying tickets on-site avoids the extra fee but totals about the same.
3. **Kopaonik Shuttle:**
Besides regular buses, many private mini-buses/shared transfers exist (Terra Travel, Gea Tours). They offer door-to-door service (Belgrade accommodation to Ski Resort). These aren't on maps; book via official websites or WhatsApp.
* **Taxi:** Use **Yandex Go** or **Pink Taxi**. Transparent pricing avoids scams.

---

## üí∞ Money and Payment
Currency is Dinar (**RSD**). Euro exchange rate is stable and Euros are often accepted. However, cash is preferred, especially by homestays/Airbnbs.

* **Strategy:** **Euro Cash** + **Multi-currency Credit Card**.
* **Exchange:** ATM/Exchange machines at airport baggage claim. Transparent rates.
* **Cash Only Scenarios:** Airport shuttle, intercity bus, taxi, some Booking payments, ski rental, coach fees, small restaurants.
* **Budget:** **500 Euros** exchanged to RSD is usually enough for two people for two weeks.

# Kopaonik Skiing Practical Advice

My advice is suitable for beginners. We didn't explore the whole mountain, mainly the beginner conveyor belts. Only went up the mountain twice with a coach and once for night skiing.
My mom (skis) and I (snowboard) are timid first-timers. We learned some lessons the hard way.

## Insurance
We want peace of mind. Suggest two types: [Travel Insurance](#travel-insurance) and [Ski Insurance](#ski-insurance).

### Travel Insurance
Hard for us: Mom came to Germany with Schengen insurance, which doesn't cover travel to Serbia (non-Schengen) for non-residents.
With Gemini's help, we found **Heymondo**, which covers travelers "already abroad". Requires 72h wait after purchase. Ideally, buy global travel insurance from China before departure.
For EU residents, annual travel insurance (e.g., Europe Assistance via Check24) is cost-effective.

### Ski Insurance
Usually included in the Ski Pass for the day. Local coverage is direct and reliable.
Frequent skiers in EU can buy [DSV (Deutsche Ski Verein)](https://www.ski-online.de/home.html). Covers global skiing risks, equipment damage/loss, and third-party liability.
Rental shops might charge extra for insurance. DSV covers this.

## Accommodation, Transport, and Rental

Plan these based on your skill level.
If staying in a town for value, you need to drive/bus, so rent lockers at the ski center.

### Accommodation
**Beginners:** Stay near **Ski Center Kopaonik** for ski-in ski-out. Beginner belts are here. Hotel **Gorski** (4-star) is famous. Homestays nearby are cheaper and have kitchen/laundry. Maxi and Idea supermarkets make life easy.
[InfoKop](https://www.infokop.net/) has all info (weather, prices, last-minute deals - Serbian only for deals, but English for lifts/slopes).

### Transport
Taxi, Ski Bus, Self-drive, Gondola.
Taxi: Pricey, potential scams.
Ski Bus: Hourly, cash only.
Gondola: Runs continuously during day.

**Experts:** Can stay in nearby villages (Brzeƒáe, Vikend Naselje) and take T-bar/lifts up. See [Chenhe's Blog](https://chenhe.me/zh/posts/skiing-in-serbia/).
**Beginners:** Don't stress yourself. Stick to Ski Center. If staying in Brzeƒáe, the new Gondola is convenient but stops at 16:00 (then you need the bus).

### Equipment Rental
Recommended: **Ma≈°inac Resort** (accommodation + restaurant). Next to Green Slope 22. Youth hostel vibe. Cheap prices: Ski 800 RSD, Snowboard 1000 RSD, Helmet/Goggles 200 RSD.

## Ski Pass and Coaches
Buy online at [InfoKop](https://www.infokop.net/) or [Official Shop](https://shop.skipas.rs/en/) for ~5% discount.
Types: Beginner (single use), Half-day, Full-day, Night, Multi-day.
Night skiing: Slope 7 (Blue, good quality, music).
Beginner slopes at Ski Center have magic carpets.
Coach prices: ~45 Euro/hr. Discounts if renting their equipment as well.
