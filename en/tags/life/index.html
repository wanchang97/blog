    Record of Insights from My Self-Practice of Yoga

    Record of Insights from My Self-Practice of Yoga: Coincidentally, I started trying Yoga during the 2020 pandemic, bought a Keep membership, and followed their hot yoga classes. After several years of consistent practice, I can indeed gradually perform many poses. The guidance from the video instructors, in my personal opinion, might surpass many in-person training classes. This is based on a simple logic I have: regardless of the Yoga instructor, they will always want to put their best side, the most detailed explanations, and the most concise content into their videos. And regardless of the person, they will inevitably slack off at work. A single in-person class usually has a relatively low real return for them. So, it’s rare for someone to teach with the mindset of ensuring you get your money’s worth; often, classes turn into listening to the teacher show off or chat. However, in the preparation of a video, on one hand, they gain higher returns due to the principle of leverage; on the other hand, even if not just for money but for reputation and sustainable development, they will surely cut out superfluous chatter and present their most professional self. I believe the same applies to other training classes, like learning German. Good, concise, and systematic online courses are not easy to prepare. For in-person classes, you just need to observe the reactions and feedback of a few students; they might come for the reputation, but more often because it’s nearby, the timing is suitable, the location is convenient, etc. However, online courses inevitably face competition from many other high-quality courses. If one doesn’t take it seriously, they won’t gain trust and followers.
